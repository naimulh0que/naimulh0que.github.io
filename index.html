I am a fourth-year Ph.D. student at the College of Information Studies (iSchool), University of Maryland, College Park. I work with Professor Niklas Elmqvist and am a member of the Human-Computer Interaction Lab. Previously, I obtained a Master's in Computer Science from Stony Brook University and a BSc in Computer Science from the University of Dhaka, Bangladesh. You can call me Naimul (pronunciation: na-ei-mul).

My research interest is designing interactive visualization for AI-infused supertools. I approach this interest in two ways. First, I design interactive visualization techniques that can adapt to large-scale data and models. For example, during my dissertation, I designed Aggregate Query Sculpting (AQS) for iteratively exploring large-scale multivariate data and Visual Concept Programming for summarizing and programming image datasets. I then investigate how the visualization techniques can work as a communication medium between humans and AI for designing supertools, systems that amplify, augment, empower, and enhance human performance, by combining user experiences with AI support. For instance, I designed a series of AI-assisted writing tools (DramatVis Personae and Portrayal) that combine visualization and NLP to support complex creative writing tasks. These tools demonstrate how to amplify human creativity with AI while retaining human agency. Please see my publication list below for more details.
